Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

January 18, 2015

Exploring My Values

The following is a list of important values to me and assessment of how I live up to those values.

ValueDegree to Which I Live Up to Value
AccomplishmentDisagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree
Close RelationshipsDisagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree
CreativityDisagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree
GrowthDisagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree
Self-knowledgeDisagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree

One value that is particularly important to me is growth. I am starting an interesting and exciting new phase professionally and personally and I am having to grow quickly. I have found that growth is not always pleasant, but I can feel that new parts of me are opening up.

The last thing the someone asked me for advice on had to do with accomplishment. A colleague at the job that I am leaving to pursue web development was considering a similar switch. She was concerned with having to start over in terms of accruing professional accomplishments in a new field. My advice was that it's far worse to stay in field where you are unsatisfied than to start over and learn something new.

When I think about my values, I feel the strongest emotional reaction from creativity and growth. My desire to create new and interesting things helps me deal with any potential stereotype threat from starting over in a new field in my 40s. I think growth and creativity are closely related in that ability. The things I'm learning about myself are both motivating and discouraging. However, I feel that encountering obstacles is an essential motivator for growth.

If I'm feeling down at DBC, I will try to utilize different resources including engaging with cohort mates, meditation, breathing techniques and writing and reflection.
