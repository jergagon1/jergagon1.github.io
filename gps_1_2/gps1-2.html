What is Pair Programming?

Pair programming is a development technique where two programmers collaborate on a single coding problem. It consists of a driver, who types the code, and a navigator who sort of guides the driver along in the right direction.The 3 main combinations of pair programming would be expert-expert, expert-novice, and novice.

Why is pair programming important to DBC? DBC wants to prepare us for "the real world" and this is a skill we will need in software development. Further, it teaches empathy for fellow programmers and enhances emotional intelligence, and it is a great learning tool. Mistakes are minimized with two sets of eyes on the program. The benfits are:

  • Economics
  • Design Quality
  • Satisfaction
  • Learning
  • Team building and Communication
